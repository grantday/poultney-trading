If you raise broilers or layers commercially, your placement schedule lives or dies on chick supply. The last two years have been instructive — and the next twelve months are going to reward farmers who plan early.

## Where we are right now

Hatchery output across the region is recovering after the bird-flu disruptions and parent-stock import bottlenecks of recent years. Capacity is largely restored, but lead times remain longer than they were pre-2023.

For broiler chick orders, our practical guidance is:

- **Book 3 to 4 weeks ahead** for orders under 500 chicks
- **Book 6 to 8 weeks ahead** for larger placements
- **Standing orders** are now the cheapest and most reliable way to secure supply

## Layer chicks behave differently

Layer chicks are tighter market. Parent stock is more specialised, hatching cycles are longer, and demand is less seasonal. Farmers building or expanding layer flocks should be talking to suppliers a full quarter before they need birds.

## What separates a good supplier

Three things to look for in any chick supplier:

- **Vaccination protocol** — ask exactly what's been administered and when
- **Sexing accuracy** — particularly for layers, where mis-sexed cockerels are pure cost
- **Mortality guarantee window** — a reputable supplier will guarantee against early-week mortality

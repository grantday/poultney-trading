Stock feed prices have been the single biggest variable on every poultry and pig farmer's spreadsheet over the last twelve months. The two big inputs — yellow maize and soya meal — together make up the majority of any balanced ration, and both have moved sharply in 2025 and into 2026.

## What's moving the price

Three forces dominate the local feed cost picture:

1. **Maize supply** — Below-average rainfall in key growing regions has tightened the local maize market. Imports help but pass through landed-cost premiums.
2. **Soya scarcity** — Domestic soya production is still well below demand. Most feed mills source on a blend of local crush and South African imports.
3. **Fuel and logistics** — Diesel pricing flows through to every tonne moved, milled and delivered.

## Practical advice for the farmer

If you're running broilers or layers, three small things meaningfully reduce your feed cost per kg of meat or per tray of eggs:

- **Reduce feed wastage at the trough** — well-designed feeders and disciplined fill levels save 5–10% of feed cost
- **Manage water quality** — poor water depresses feed conversion ratios faster than most farmers realise
- **Buy on schedule, not on panic** — buying when you're nearly out is the most expensive way to buy feed
